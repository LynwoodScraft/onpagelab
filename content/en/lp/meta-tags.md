## Optimize Your On-Page SEO Meta Tags

The title tag and meta description are the first elements users see in search results. Their optimization combines technical precision (pixel length) and copywriting (encouraging clicks). OnPageLab analyzes both dimensions simultaneously.

**CTA:** [Check my meta tags →] → /en/seo-tool/

## Why pixel length, not character count?

Google measures the display space for a title in **pixels**, not characters. A lowercase "i" doesn't take up the same space as an uppercase "M". Two 60-character titles can therefore be truncated differently based on their composition.

OnPageLab uses a reference font identical to Google's to calculate pixel width with 95%+ accuracy.

---

## What OnPageLab checks

**Title tag:** pixel length (optimal 500–590 px), keyword at beginning, brand separator, forbidden characters, duplication.

**Meta description:** pixel length (optimal 430–920 px), call to action presence, keyword inclusion, uniqueness, double quotes detection.

**Open Graph:** og:title, og:description, og:image (presence and size), og:url coherence.

**Twitter Card:** twitter:card type, image and description presence.

## FAQ

**Does Google always use my title and meta description?**
Not always. Google rewrites titles in about 60% of cases and meta descriptions about 70% of the time. A well-crafted title gets rewritten less often.

**Does the meta description affect rankings?**
Not directly. Google confirmed meta descriptions are not a ranking signal. However, a well-written meta description improves CTR, which sends positive behavioral signals.
